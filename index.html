DMD Hub home page

UConn Athletics Champions Hall

Motion Graphics | Photoshop

Men's basketball example

Women's basketball example

I worked in a team to design 30 motion graphics and mockups of the 2021-2022 UConn Men's and Women's Basketball Teams. These graphics were to be displayed on two-story tall LED boards in the Werth Family UConn Basketball Champions Center. The videos for each respective team have the same layout and format, but the photos and information, such as name and hometown, are adapted for each player. The examples above show the slight differences between the men's and women's videos.

For our final deliverables, we submitted two google drive folders for both teams. Within these folders included Photoshop templates, After Effects templates, project files, assets, and, most importantly, final motion graphics videos for each specific player.

My team and I communicated weekly with UConn clients on zoom or in person and presented mockups and received any feedback. Throughout this project, I embraced teamwork and gained excellent communication skills with my team members and clients. This experience was in collaboration with UConn senior DMD class Agency.

Curtain Call - Ticketing App Prototype

UI/UX Design

Curtain Call screens

Curtain Call was created to provide a way for theater goers to easily buy last minute tickets at a discounted price online. The app would use your current location on your phone or device so the system can determine if you are eligible to buy a ticket. This project aimed to not only increase overall sales for shows, but the accessibility for local theatergoers will influence more customers to purchase more tickets in the future.

The app was designed in a process of three sprints, each sprint adding new functionality and features. I also conducted needfinding research through a 10 question google form and had over 30 responses.

The first sprint focused heavily on the design aspects such as logo, name, and color palette. I also worked on the login screens during this time. The second sprint included fixing the functionality and working a lot on the main part of the app, buying tickets. This included an about page for each show, seat views, and the availability of tickets. The third sprint was all about fine tuning what I had already created, while also finishing the profile and favorites screens. Within this sprint I built: favorites page, profile page, a separate ticketing screen just for rush tickets, search screen, and QR code screen for purchased tickets.

CTDA Social Justice Collection

Graphic Design | Logo Design

CTDA Social Justice Collection home page

The Connecticut Digital Archive is part of the Digital Preservation Repository Program at the University of Connecticut. They serve the entire state and are dedicated to the maintenance, delivery, and preservation of a wide range of digital resources for educational and cultural institutions and State Agencies in Connecticut.

For this project, the CTDA wanted to create thematic collections to highlight important content that was sometimes hidden or more difficult to find within the repository. I was tasked with designing a logo for the project and then stylizing the thematic images in a compelling way.

When making the logo, the CTDA team and I talked about how we wanted to amplify certain collections, such as social justice and women's suffrage. I wanted this idea to translate in the logo, so I designed different variations primarily with a spotlight or sign. We then wanted the thematic collections to be called "featured topics" so that we could include more content, but decided that a spotlight was a good way to combine both ideas. Using colors from the CTDA style guide, I built out many versions, before deciding on the final layout. As a team, we then collaborated on the final details, including colors, layout, and font size. The final logo below will be used for each feature topic collection and the word(s) in the spotlight we coincide with each collection.

Final logo

For the thumbnail images, I again used the colors from the CTDA style guide and overlaid them on certain parts of the images, for example, making the bus yellow in the image below. These edits made the images stand out more within the collection and allowed the page to match the CTDA branding.

Levo International Website Update

Web Design

Levo International Home Page

In the Spring of 2022, I had the opportunity to work with Levo International, which creates food security solutions to build stronger communities in Connecticut and Haiti through partnership and innovation. A team of four other students and I were tasked with increasing their brand awareness through cohesive and professional content on both social media and their Squarespace website.

One other student and I completed updates on their website which primarily focused on cleaning up the site and making sure all content was easily legible and accessible. Throughout our analysis of the site, we noticed pages where content was overlapping or displayed in unfavorable ways and wanted to fix these issues.

Before and after of the header on the web version

Before and after of the header on the mobile version

On every page we fixed spacing issues, overlapping text, button size and color consistency, and link legibility. The header and footer were also primary issues because they were overlapping and not completely responsive. For the header, I added links to highlight on hover, a hamburger menu icon for the mobile version, and shopping bag because Levo wanted to focus on their products. For the footer I made it responsive by adding columns and again added links and social media logos to highlight on hover. Finally, we added and cleaned up their member interest forms to increase volunteering, added previews of updated instagram and twitter posts, and cleaned up the events page through spacing and content hierarchy.

Digital Sign

Javascipt | Web Design

Morning screen example 1

Morning screen example 2

This digital sign landing page was created with Javascript, HTML and CSS. The site includes accurate date and time, background images from Unsplash API which change based on time of day and refreshing the browser, greetings based on time of day, and the ability to change the name of the user through local storage. Additionally, the site has a progress bar to represent the 24 hours in a day and a place the user can add and check off daily goals.
